Case study

Olist e‑commerce customer behavior analysis

Analyzed 100k+ Brazilian e‑commerce orders to understand revenue, delivery performance, and customer behavior, then turned the findings into a Power BI dashboard for decision‑makers.

Tools Excel · SQL · Power BI
Scope Order, customer, seller & delivery data
Dataset size 100k+ orders
Overview

From 100k raw orders to a decision‑ready view of Olist

The goal was to move beyond “big table of transactions” and build a clear picture of which customers, categories, and sellers actually drive revenue and satisfaction.

I combined multiple Olist tables (orders, items, products, customers, sellers, payments, reviews, and geolocation) to create a single view across the customer journey: purchase, payment, shipment, delivery, and review.

Business questions

What the business needed to know

  • Which product categories generate most revenue.
  • How delivery delays impact review scores and satisfaction.
  • Which states concentrate the highest number of customers.
  • Which sellers contribute the largest share of revenue.
  • What drives average order value across customers and categories.
Analysis performed

How I approached the analysis

  • SQL joins across 8+ tables to build a clean, analysis‑ready model.
  • Revenue trend analysis by month, category, and seller.
  • Delivery performance analysis using difference between promised and actual delivery dates.
  • Customer distribution by state and region.
  • Payment behavior breakdown by method and installments.
  • Seller performance comparison by revenue, orders, and review scores.
Key insights

What the dashboard revealed

  • A small group of sellers contributes a disproportionate share of total revenue.
  • Orders with late deliveries show noticeably lower review scores.
  • Customers strongly prefer credit card installments as a payment method.
  • Revenue is concentrated in a few high‑performing product categories.
Role & responsibilities

End‑to‑end: data cleaning, SQL modeling, exploratory analysis, and Power BI dashboard design, plus summarizing findings into a short story non‑technical stakeholders can follow.
