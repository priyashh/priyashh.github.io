Case Study // E-Commerce

Olist customer behavior analysis.

From 100,000+ raw Brazilian e-commerce orders to a decision-ready Power BI dashboard.

SQL Power BI Excel

Creating a clear picture of Olist's revenue

The goal was to move beyond a "big table of transactions" and build a clear picture of which customers, categories, and sellers actually drive revenue and satisfaction.

I combined multiple Olist tables (orders, items, products, customers, sellers, payments, reviews, and geolocation) to create a single view across the customer journey: purchase, payment, shipment, delivery, and review.

The business questions

  • Which product categories generate the most revenue?
  • How do delivery delays impact review scores and customer satisfaction?
  • Which states concentrate the highest number of customers?
  • Which sellers contribute the largest share of overall revenue?

Key insights revealed

  • Seller Concentration: A significantly small group of sellers contributes a disproportionate share of total revenue.
  • Logistics impact: Orders with late deliveries show noticeably lower review scores, proving shipping speed is tied directly to brand perception.
  • Payment Preferences: Customers strongly prefer credit card installments as their primary payment method.

Role & Responsibilities

End-to-end project execution: data cleaning, SQL modeling, exploratory analysis, and Power BI dashboard design. Summarized findings for non-technical stakeholders.
