Project Overview

Olist is Brazil's largest department store marketplace, connecting small businesses across the country to major e-commerce channels through a single contract. This dataset — publicly available on Kaggle — contains real anonymized commercial data from 2016 to 2018, spanning orders, payments, products, sellers, customers, and reviews across 8 relational tables.

The goal of this project was to perform a comprehensive exploratory data analysis (EDA) to understand the business: what's driving revenue, where delivery is falling short, which regions dominate, and what customers actually think.

Problem Statement

Olist operates a distributed multi-seller model across a geographically vast country. Key business questions for this analysis:

  • Which product categories and states drive the most revenue?
  • How does delivery performance vary across regions, and what's the impact on customer satisfaction?
  • What are the dominant payment methods and order patterns?
  • Where are the concentration risks — among sellers, geographies, and categories?

Methodology

The project followed a structured data analysis pipeline — from raw data ingestion through to visualisation and insight generation.

  • Data Loading & Merging: Eight CSV tables joined using Pandas on order_id, product_id, customer_id, and seller_id keys.
  • Cleaning & Validation: Handled null values in delivery dates, standardised category names using the translation table, parsed timestamps.
  • SQL Queries: Used SQL to segment orders by status, compute on-time delivery rates, aggregate revenue by state and category.
  • EDA: Distribution analysis for review scores, delivery days, payment types, and geographic concentration.
  • Power BI Dashboard: Built an interactive multi-page dashboard with slicers for state, category, time period, and order status.
  • Presentation: Synthesised findings into a 10-slide report with actionable recommendations.

Key Findings

💰
Revenue: R$16M+ across 2 years
Bed/Bath/Table leads at R$1.71M. Top 5 categories account for ~50% of total revenue, indicating healthy diversification.
🗺️
SP dominates with 47.5% of revenue
Southeast Brazil (SP+RJ+MG) contributes ~79% of all revenue. Northern and Central-West regions remain largely untapped.
🚚
91.9% on-time delivery rate
Average delivery is 12.1 days. Most orders (55%) arrive within 6–15 days. Late deliveries correlate directly with 1-star reviews.
⭐
4.09/5 average review score
57% of reviews are 5-star. The 11.4% 1-star rate is almost entirely tied to late or missing deliveries — a directly addressable problem.
💳
Credit card = 78% of revenue
Boleto (Brazilian bank slip) handles 18%. Strong installment-plan culture influences purchase decisions and cart sizes.
⚠️
Seller concentration risk
Top 10% of sellers drive ~65% of order volume. SP holds 22% of all sellers. Platform is overly dependent on a small seller group.
📌
Late deliveries are the #1 driver of negative reviews. Orders delivered more than 5 days past the estimated date had a review score average below 2.5, versus 4.5+ for on-time deliveries.
📌
~4,577 orders took 30+ days to deliver — these represent the highest-risk segment for customer churn and are concentrated in Northern states (AM, PA).
📌
South/Southeast holds 85% of sellers. Onboarding sellers in the North would directly reduce delivery times for those regions, improving both logistics performance and review scores simultaneously.

Recommendations

  • Logistics partnerships in Northern Brazil: Partner with regional carriers to cut delivery times in AM, PA, MA — the primary late-delivery states.
  • Proactive delivery alerts: Implement SMS/email notifications when orders are at risk of being late. This reduces negative reviews even when delays occur.
  • Seller diversification programme: Incentivise new sellers in underrepresented states to reduce concentration risk and improve regional logistics.
  • Category expansion campaigns: Sports, toys, and electronics show strong growth potential and currently under-index vs. their market size.
  • Instalment-optimised promotions: With 78% credit card usage, promotional campaigns around 0% instalment plans during off-peak months could drive volume.

What I Learned

  • Working with relational multi-table datasets — managing joins across 8 tables without losing data integrity was the core technical challenge.
  • How to build DAX measures in Power BI for dynamic filtering across slicers (state, category, time period).
  • The importance of geographic analysis in any marketplace business — regional patterns often explain anomalies that aggregate stats hide.
  • How to translate EDA findings into a structured business narrative that non-technical stakeholders can act on.
