The Goal

Finding the real story in a mountain of e-commerce data

I started this project with eight massive datasets filled with over a hundred thousand raw e-commerce orders from Brazil. The first step was using SQL to connect everything together so I could actually see how customers sellers and payments all interacted with each other. Once the data was cleaned up and actually made sense I brought it all into Power BI. I wanted to build something that could answer the real questions a business owner cares about like where the money is actually coming from why customers leave bad reviews and who the best sellers really are.

The final result is a four part visual dashboard that breaks down the entire business into clear categories like revenue delivery operations customer behavior and seller intelligence.

Dashboard 01

Following the money across categories and states

This first dashboard breaks down exactly where the revenue comes from. It turns out Health and Beauty is the single biggest money maker on the platform followed closely by home goods and watches. When it comes to how people actually pay for these things credit cards absolutely dominate the platform handling almost eighty percent of all the money spent.

We also found something really interesting about how people use installment plans because the more expensive an item is the more likely a customer is to stretch their payments out over ten months. Geographically the state of São Paulo is the absolute powerhouse of the business generating more money than the next four states combined.

Dashboard 02

How late deliveries instantly ruin customer trust

The most revealing part of this entire project is the chart showing how delivery times impact customer reviews. The pattern is absolutely brutal. If an order arrives even just a few days early customers consistently leave glowing four or five star reviews. But the exact moment a package becomes even one day late the average review score completely falls off a cliff and drops into the twos.

This shows that customers do not forgive lateness no matter how good the actual product is. Overall the platform does a pretty good job with a 93 percent on time delivery rate but that late delivery penalty is a massive risk for the business.

Dashboard 03

The massive challenge of getting customers to come back

This page looks at who is actually buying all these products and it uncovers the biggest problem the business faces. Almost 97 percent of all customers place exactly one order and never come back. This means the company is constantly forced to spend money finding brand new customers because they have almost zero loyalty or repeat buyers.

Just like with the revenue São Paulo is carrying the entire platform with over 40 thousand unique customers while most other states barely make a dent. Relying so heavily on just one state makes the whole business incredibly vulnerable to local economic problems.

Dashboard 04

Finding the true top performers on the platform

The final piece of the puzzle is looking at the people actually selling the products. Out of three thousand different sellers on the platform just a handful are doing the heavy lifting. The absolute best seller handled over eighteen hundred individual orders all by themselves while another top seller made even more money by focusing on expensive premium items instead of just selling a high volume of cheap things.

We also noticed that while sellers in São Paulo push out the most volume the sellers in other states actually get much better customer reviews. It proves that selling the most items does not always mean you are providing the best service.

Key Findings

The biggest takeaways from the data

Late delivery is a death sentence

Even just a few days late destroys review scores. Early delivery is the only way to guarantee happy customers on this platform.

Zero customer loyalty

With 97 percent of people buying only once the platform is basically surviving on a constant treadmill of finding new buyers.

São Paulo carries the team

Whether it is total customers sellers or pure revenue this single state completely dominates the entire e-commerce business.

Installments are for big purchases

People are not using payment plans for small impulse buys. They save the ten month payment plans for expensive high ticket items.

High volume does not mean high quality

The sellers moving the most products actually have worse customer satisfaction scores than smaller sellers in other states.

Methodology

How I actually built this

I started by downloading eight different raw data files containing details on nearly a hundred thousand orders. I used SQL to clean up the messy data connect the customers to their payments and calculate exactly how long each delivery took.

Once I had everything linked together I exported the numbers into Excel to double check the math and look for early trends. Finally I loaded the clean data directly into Power BI where I designed all the interactive charts and built the complete four page dashboard.
