The Goal

Mapping the journey

I started this project with six massive spreadsheets of raw data covering everything from daily stock prices and yearly earnings to company milestones. The first thing I had to do was organize all of this mess so I used PostgreSQL to connect all the pieces together and find the hidden patterns. Once the data was clean and actually made sense I brought it all into Power BI.

I wanted to build something that could answer the real questions people actually care about like how did a ten thousand dollar investment turn into over a million dollars or why did the stock crash during certain years and is the company actually making more money today than it used to.

Alphabet at a glance

This first dashboard gives you a high level view of the four main numbers that tell the story of Google over the last two decades. Right now the stock price sits at 359 dollars. That might sound a little low if you remember the stock trading in the thousands but that is just because the company split its stock a couple of times. The most important number on this whole page is the 25.6 percent revenue CAGR.

The stock price chart shows the entire journey from 2004 all the way to 2025 where you can see the long steady climb upward. Back in 2004 they were barely making anything compared to today. They finally crossed the 100 billion dollar mark in 2018 and by 2025 they were bringing in a staggering 385 billion dollars a year.

Google holds the ground

The first chart tracks the stock price using something called Simple Moving Averages or SMA. During big market panics like the 2008 financial crisis and the 2022 market drop they drifted far apart. In 2022 the shorter 50 day average actually crossed below the longer 200 day average. Traders call this a Death Cross because it usually signals a really bad time for the stock.

The next chart looks at something called Drawdown. A drawdown just measures how far the stock price has fallen from its absolute highest point. In the early days of the company the stock would regularly drop 60 to 80 percent from its peak. As the business grew bigger and more stable those massive drops happened less often.

Context on 2022: Why did the stock drop 39% that year? It wasn't fundamentally a Google problem. The entire tech sector took a massive hit because the Federal Reserve was aggressively hiking interest rates.

How the company actually makes its money

This dashboard breaks down exactly where all that money comes from. For a long time Google Search was basically the entire business and it still brings in about 59 percent of the total revenue today. But as the company grew they started relying more on other things like YouTube ads and their massive Cloud business.

The most exciting part of this whole page is the Cloud business. Back in 2017 it was only bringing in about 4 billion dollars a year which sounds like a lot but was basically nothing for a company this size. By 2025 that number skyrocketed to 46 billion dollars.

Looking under the hood at the financial health

This final dashboard looks at the overall health of the business to see if they are actually keeping the money they make. One of the craziest things about this company is their gross margin which has stayed around 60 percent for two decades. That basically means for every dollar they make it only costs them about 40 cents to deliver the service.

The last big takeaway is their free cash flow. This is the actual cash left over after they pay for everything including massive investments in new computer servers and AI technology. By 2025 they had 72 billion dollars in pure cash left over.

The biggest takeaways from the data

Every crash recovered

Every major crash like 2008 or 2022 was fully recovered within a year and a half. People who just held onto the stock were always rewarded.

Cloud is the new engine

The Cloud business is no longer a side project. It grew ten times larger in just eight years and is becoming a massive part of their future profits.

Healthy diversification

Google Search brings in a smaller piece of the total pie today but that is actually a good thing. It means the company is successfully building other massive businesses like YouTube so they don't have to rely on just one thing.

Massive cash reserves

The company generates so much pure cash that they can afford to spend billions on new AI servers while still paying their investors a dividend without taking on any debt.

Panic vs reality

The biggest stock drops happened because of global economic panic and not because the actual business was failing. During the 2022 crash the company was actually still growing its revenue.

Methodology

I started by gathering six different spreadsheets filled with over two decades of financial reports and daily stock prices. I used PostgreSQL to link all these different files together and write custom queries to find the hidden trends and calculate things like growth rates and volatility.

Once the data was organized I brought it into Excel to double check the math and plan out the story. Finally I connected the database straight into Power BI where I designed the interactive charts and built the final four page dashboard.
