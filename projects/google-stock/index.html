Project Overview

This project explores the historical stock price data for Alphabet Inc. (Google, ticker: GOOGL) using Python's data science ecosystem. The goal was to practice time-series analysis techniques — identifying trends, computing rolling statistics, and visualising price movement patterns that are meaningful to both technical and non-technical audiences.

Rather than predicting future prices, this is a descriptive and exploratory analysis: understanding what happened, when, and why it matters for analysts working with financial data.

Problem Statement

Key questions explored in this project:

  • What does Google's long-term price trajectory look like, and when were the major inflection points?
  • How do 50-day and 200-day moving averages signal trend shifts (Golden Cross / Death Cross)?
  • How has daily return volatility changed across different periods?
  • What does the distribution of daily returns reveal about the stock's risk profile?

Methodology

  • Data Source: Yahoo Finance historical data via yfinance or a pre-downloaded CSV covering multi-year GOOGL price history.
  • Pandas: Loaded, cleaned, and indexed the time series. Computed daily returns, rolling means, and standard deviations.
  • Moving Averages: Computed 50-day and 200-day simple moving averages to identify trend direction and crossover signals.
  • Volatility Analysis: Plotted rolling standard deviation of returns to identify periods of market turbulence (COVID crash, 2022 selloff).
  • Return Distribution: Plotted histogram of daily percentage returns to assess normality and tail risk.
  • Matplotlib / Seaborn: Created multi-panel visualisations showing price, volume, moving averages, and volatility together.

Key Findings

📈
Long-term uptrend is clear
GOOGL's price has grown dramatically over 20 years, with major acceleration phases post-2012 and post-2020.
⚡
COVID crash & recovery
March 2020 saw a sharp volatility spike followed by one of the fastest recoveries in the stock's history.
📉
2022 selloff was significant
Rising interest rates triggered a prolonged downtrend. The 200-day MA acted as a reliable resistance level.
🎯
Moving averages worked well
Golden Cross (50MA crossing above 200MA) consistently preceded multi-month rallies. Death Cross preceded corrections.
📌
Daily return distribution is approximately normal with slight negative skew — consistent with most large-cap stocks. Fat tails (outlier days) represent significant events (earnings, macro news).
📌
Volume spikes reliably precede price movement. High volume days often mark trend reversals or breakouts — a useful signal for further analysis.
📌
Rolling 30-day volatility clustered around major events — COVID (March 2020), Fed rate decisions (2022-23), and earnings releases. This aligns with the GARCH volatility clustering hypothesis.

What I Learned

  • How to work with datetime-indexed DataFrames in Pandas — resampling, rolling windows, and period-based filtering.
  • The mechanics of moving average crossover signals and why they matter in practice for trend identification.
  • How to build multi-panel financial charts in Matplotlib that tell a coherent visual story.
  • How volatility clustering works empirically — and why standard deviation of returns changes dramatically across market regimes.
