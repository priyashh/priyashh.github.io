The Goal

Mapping the journey

I started this project with six massive spreadsheets of raw data covering everything from daily stock prices and yearly earnings to company milestones. The first thing I had to do was organize all of this mess so I used a database tool called PostgreSQL to connect all the pieces together and find the hidden patterns. Once the data was clean and actually made sense I brought it all into Power BI which is just a software used to build interactive visual charts. I wanted to build something that could answer the real questions people actually care about like how did a ten thousand dollar investment turn into over a million dollars or why did the stock crash during certain years and is the company actually making more money today than it used to.

The final result is a four part visual dashboard that breaks down the entire twenty one year journey of the company. It walks you through a high level summary then dives deep into the stock price history the different ways the company makes money and finally the overall financial health of the business.

Alphabet at a glance

This first dashboard gives you a high level view of the four main numbers that tell the story of Google over the last two decades. Right now the stock price sits at 359 dollars. That might sound a little low if you remember the stock trading in the thousands but that is just because the company split its stock a couple of times. They basically chopped the expensive shares into smaller cheaper pieces so more normal people could afford to buy them. The most important number on this whole page is the 25.6 percent revenue CAGR. CAGR is just a fancy financial term that stands for Compound Annual Growth Rate. It basically means the company grew the money it brings in by over 25 percent every single year on average. To put that into perspective the overall stock market usually only grows by about 10 percent a year so Google was absolutely crushing the average.

The stock price chart shows the entire journey from 2004 all the way to 2025 where you can see the long steady climb upward. The revenue chart next to it tells an even clearer story of just how massive this company became. Back in 2004 they were barely making anything compared to today. They finally crossed the 100 billion dollar mark in 2018 and by 2025 they were bringing in a staggering 385 billion dollars a year.

Google holds the ground

The first chart tracks the stock price using something called Simple Moving Averages or SMA. An SMA is just the average price of the stock over a set amount of time like 50 days or 200 days. Looking at an average instead of daily prices smooths out the chaotic daily jumps so you can actually see the real trend. Most of the time the 50 day average and the 200 day average track pretty close together but during big market panics like the 2008 financial crisis and the 2022 market drop they drifted far apart. In 2022 the shorter 50 day average actually crossed below the longer 200 day average. Traders call this a Death Cross because it usually signals a really bad time for the stock. Sure enough that same year the stock had its worst year on record and dropped almost 39 percent. But here is the bright side because every time the stock crashed like this it recovered and hit brand new record highs within a year and a half.

The next chart looks at something called Drawdown. A drawdown just measures how far the stock price has fallen from its absolute highest point. The chart uses a white line to show these drops and it really puts the risk into perspective. In the early days of the company the stock would regularly drop 60 to 80 percent from its peak. As the business grew bigger and more stable those massive drops happened less often. However even recently in 2022 it still dropped 70 percent from its top before climbing back up. This shows that anyone wanting to hold this stock for the long haul needs to be prepared for some scary drops along the way.

The last part shows the yearly returns and the best single days for the stock. You can really see the massive ups and downs here. The stock completely doubled in value during 2004 and 2007 but then lost over half its value in 2008. It had a nice steady run of winning years from 2010 to 2021 before taking a big hit in 2022 and bouncing back again. We also included a table of the top 10 best days the stock ever had. On the absolute best day recently the stock price jumped by 59 dollars in just one day. These massive single day jumps almost always happen right after an earnings surprise which is just when the company publicly announces they made a lot more money than experts thought they would.

Context on 2022: Why did the stock drop 39% that year? It wasn't fundamentally a Google problem. The entire tech sector took a massive hit because the Federal Reserve was aggressively hiking interest rates, and companies started slashing their digital ad budgets out of fear of a looming recession.

Dashboard 03

How the company actually makes its money

This dashboard breaks down exactly where all that money comes from. For a long time Google Search was basically the entire business and it still brings in about 59 percent of the total revenue today. But as the company grew they started relying more on other things like YouTube ads and their massive Cloud business.

The middle chart shows how much the revenue changed from one year to the next. The biggest jump happened right around 2021 when everyone was stuck at home during the pandemic and businesses poured money into online ads. That single year added 75 billion dollars to their total revenue.

The most exciting part of this whole page is the Cloud business. Back in 2017 it was only bringing in about 4 billion dollars a year which sounds like a lot but was basically nothing for a company this size. By 2025 that number skyrocketed to 46 billion dollars. Cloud used to cost them money to run but now it is a massive profit engine that is growing incredibly fast.

Dashboard 04

Looking under the hood at the financial health

This final dashboard looks at the overall health of the business to see if they are actually keeping the money they make. One of the craziest things about this company is their gross margin which has stayed around 60 percent for two decades. That basically means for every dollar they make it only costs them about 40 cents to deliver the service. This is the beauty of a digital software business because adding a new user costs almost nothing.

We also looked at how efficient the company is with its money and if they have enough cash on hand to cover their bills. The little gauge chart at the bottom shows a current ratio of 3 which simply means they have three times as much cash as they need to pay off their short term debts. They are in absolutely no danger of going broke.

The last big takeaway is their free cash flow. This is the actual cash left over after they pay for everything including massive investments in new computer servers and AI technology. By 2025 they had 72 billion dollars in pure cash left over. They use this massive pile of money to buy back their own stock and pay dividends to their investors.

Key Findings

The biggest takeaways from the data

Every crash recovered

Every major crash like 2008 or 2022 was fully recovered within a year and a half. People who just held onto the stock were always rewarded.

Cloud is the new engine

The Cloud business is no longer a side project. It grew ten times larger in just eight years and is becoming a massive part of their future profits.

Healthy diversification

Google Search brings in a smaller piece of the total pie today but that is actually a good thing. It means the company is successfully building other massive businesses like YouTube so they don't have to rely on just one thing.

Massive cash reserves

The company generates so much pure cash that they can afford to spend billions on new AI servers while still paying their investors a dividend without taking on any debt.

Panic vs reality

The biggest stock drops happened because of global economic panic and not because the actual business was failing. During the 2022 crash the company was actually still growing its revenue.

Methodology

How I actually built this

I started by gathering six different spreadsheets filled with over two decades of financial reports and daily stock prices. I used PostgreSQL to link all these different files together and write custom queries to find the hidden trends and calculate things like growth rates and volatility.

Once the data was organized I brought it into Excel to double check the math and plan out the story. Finally I connected the database straight into Power BI where I designed the interactive charts and built the final four page dashboard.
