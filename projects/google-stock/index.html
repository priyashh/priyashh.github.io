Excel Financial Data Volatility Analysis

Exploring Google’s stock behavior over time

The goal of this project was to practice working with time‑based financial data in Excel and understand how simple visuals can reveal trends and volatility.

Starting from a historical price dataset, I cleaned and organized the columns (date, open, high, low, close, volume) and built charts that highlight overall movement, local swings, and periods of higher volatility.

What I analyzed

  • Price trend over time using line charts on closing prices to identify macro trajectories.
  • Average price movement across selected periods to smooth out daily noise.
  • Volatility measurements to understand how much the price fluctuates day to day and identify high-risk market environments.

Key learnings

  • Working comfortably with time‑based data (sorting by date, handling gaps, choosing the right date granularity).
  • Building clear trend charts that show long‑term direction while maintaining visibility on short‑term swings.
  • Interpreting price movement patterns and relating spikes or drops to broader market context.

Role & Focus

Solo project focused on exploring stock data in Excel, building charts, and describing what the visuals say in simple, non‑technical language.

Dataset Details

Source: Historical Google stock prices.
Type: Time-series (daily prices).
